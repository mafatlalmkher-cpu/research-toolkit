At the bench

Tissue-culture media calculator

Plant growth regulators are the fiddly part of making a medium. These tools convert between mg·L⁻¹ and µM, work out how much stock to pipette, and scale a recipe to any volume, using molecular weights for the regulators you actually use.

How much stock to add

Pick a regulator, the concentration you want in the medium, the batch volume, and your stock strength.

–

Volume of stock = (target mg·L⁻¹ × medium L) ÷ stock mg·mL⁻¹. A 1 mg·mL⁻¹ stock is convenient: 1 mL gives 1 mg·L⁻¹ in 1 L of medium.

mg·L⁻¹ ↔ µM

Concentration interconversion for one regulator. Edit either box.

↔

µM = mg·L⁻¹ × 1000 ÷ MW. mg·L⁻¹ = µM × MW ÷ 1000.

Prepare a stock solution

How much to weigh for a stock, and how to dissolve it.

–

Weigh the shown mass, dissolve in the minimum volume of the indicated solvent, then make up to the stock volume with water. Store cold and dark; note which regulators are heat-labile and should be filter-sterilised rather than autoclaved.
