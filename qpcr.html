qPCR / RT-qPCR analysis

Two calculations that come up in every gene-expression or diagnostics run: relative expression from cycle thresholds, and primer efficiency from a dilution series. Enter your Ct values; the maths runs on your device.

Relative expression

One row per biological sample. Put technical replicate Ct values in a cell separated by spaces or commas; they are averaged. Choose which group is the calibrator (control).

SampleGroupTarget CtReference Ct

Per sample, ΔCt = mean target Ct − mean reference Ct. ΔΔCt = group mean ΔCt − calibrator mean ΔCt. Fold change = 2^(−ΔΔCt) (Livak & Schmittgen, 2001). The range comes from ±1 SD of the group's ΔCt. When efficiencies are not set to 100%, an efficiency-corrected ratio (Pfaffl, 2001) is also shown.

Primer efficiency from a standard curve

Efficiency = 10^(−1/slope) − 1, from a linear fit of Ct against log₁₀(template amount). A slope of −3.32 corresponds to 100% efficiency (the amount doubles each cycle). Acceptable assays usually fall between −3.1 and −3.6 (90–110%) with R² ≥ 0.98.

Part of the Research Toolkit · free & open source (MIT). Methods: Livak & Schmittgen, Methods 2001; Pfaffl, Nucleic Acids Res 2001. Calculations are standard but unvalidated for clinical use; confirm against your instrument's software for reportable results.
