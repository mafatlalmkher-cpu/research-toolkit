Nucleic-acid quantification (A260)

    
Convert a spectrophotometer reading into concentration using the Beer–Lambert relationship for nucleic acids.

    

      

      

      

      

      

    

    
–

    
Concentration = A260 × factor × dilution. The factor is the µg·mL⁻¹ that gives an absorbance of 1.0 in a 1 cm path: 50 for dsDNA, 33 for ssDNA, 40 for RNA. µg·mL⁻¹ equals ng·µL⁻¹. A260/A280 near 1.8 (DNA) or 2.0 (RNA) and A260/A230 of 2.0–2.2 indicate clean preparations.

  


  
  

    
Mass ↔ moles ↔ copies

    
Convert between mass and amount of substance for a nucleic acid of known length. Enter a length and one amount; the rest are computed.

    

      

      

      

      

      

    

    
–

    
Edit mass or amount and the other follows. Molecular weight ≈ length × (650 dsDNA / 330 ssDNA / 340 ssRNA). One µg of 1 kb dsDNA ≈ 1.52 pmol. Copy number = moles × 6.022×10²³.

  


  
  

    
Oligonucleotide molecular weight

    
For a synthetic single-stranded DNA oligo (5′-OH, no modifications).

    
    
–

    

      

      

    

    
–
Volume of water/TE to add for the target stock concentration.

    
MW = (nA·313.21 + nT·304.20 + nC·289.18 + nG·329.21) − 61.96 g/mol (anhydrous, IDT convention). To make a stock: volume (µL) = nmol ÷ µM × 1000.
