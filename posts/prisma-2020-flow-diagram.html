Reporting your review: the PRISMA 2020 flow diagram

The flow diagram is where a reader checks that your review is complete and reproducible. The numbers have to add up.

20 June 2026 · 2 min read

The PRISMA 2020 flow diagram is a small picture that carries a lot of weight. It tells the reader, and the reviewer, how many records you started with, what you removed and why, and how many studies survived to the final synthesis. A diagram whose numbers do not reconcile signals a problem with the review, so it is worth getting right.

What each stage means

The diagram has three bands. Identification counts the records retrieved from databases and registers, and the records removed before screening, mostly duplicates. Screening counts the records screened on title and abstract, those excluded, the reports sought for full text, those not retrieved, the reports assessed for eligibility, and those excluded with reasons. Included counts the studies that made it into the review.

The counts have to reconcile

Each box is fixed by the ones above it. Records screened equals records identified minus those removed. Reports sought equals records screened minus those excluded. Reports assessed equals reports sought minus those not retrieved. Studies included equals reports assessed minus the full-text exclusions. If any of these does not subtract cleanly, the diagram is wrong, and a reader will notice.

Common mistakes

  • Numbers that do not subtract to the next box, usually from a miscounted exclusion total.
  • Listing full-text exclusion reasons that do not sum to the number excluded.
  • Forgetting the second arm when some studies came from citation searching, websites, or contacting authors.
