Why we do not bundle a screener

After de-duplication you decide which records to keep from their titles and abstracts. Free tools already do this well, including two-reviewer workflows and machine-learning prioritisation that a single web page cannot match. So the toolkit recommends the best free tool rather than competing with a thinner copy. Build and de-duplicate your search in the toolkit, then screen in one of the tools below; your records export as RIS or CSV, which all three import.

Which one should you use

  1. No setup, any device: Catchii. Free, no account, no install, runs in a browser on a phone or laptop, and covers de-duplication, screening, and the PRISMA flow. The closest fit for limited bandwidth or no rights to install software.
  2. Most powerful, if you can install software: ASReview. Validated machine-learning screening that ranks records by likely relevance and saves time on large sets. Needs Python or Docker. If you use it, cite van de Schoot et al. (2021), Nature Machine Intelligence 3:125–133, doi:10.1038/s42256-020-00287-7.
  3. Two reviewers together: Rayyan. Web-based blind screening with conflict resolution. The free tier covers screening; an account is required and some features are paid.

How it fits the workflow

Build your search in QueryBee, de-duplicate with the toolkit's de-duplicator, screen in the tool you chose, then record the numbers in the PRISMA 2020 flow diagram. The whole path stays free.
