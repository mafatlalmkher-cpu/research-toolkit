What it does

Searching Scopus, Web of Science, and PubMed for the same topic returns many of the same papers. This tool loads your exports, matches records first by DOI and then by near-identical title, groups the duplicates so you can confirm every merge, and exports one clean, combined library.

How to use it

  1. Export your results from each database as RIS, BibTeX, or CSV.
  2. Drop all the files in at once, or paste the text.
  3. Click Find duplicates.
  4. Review each group. The most complete record is kept by default; click another to keep it instead.
  5. Export the de-duplicated set as RIS, BibTeX, or CSV.

Good to know

Everything runs in your browser, so even unpublished or sensitive libraries never leave your machine. The similarity threshold is adjustable if you want stricter or looser title matching.
