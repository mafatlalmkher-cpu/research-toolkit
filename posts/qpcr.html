qPCR / RT-qPCR analysis

Relative expression and primer efficiency without setting up a spreadsheet.

What it does

Two calculations from every gene-expression run. The first gives relative expression by the 2^-ΔΔCt method, and by the Pfaffl method when efficiencies are not 100%, with replicates and fold change. The second fits a dilution-series standard curve and reports the slope, efficiency, and R².

How to use it

  1. For expression, enter one row per sample with its target and reference Ct values (replicates separated by spaces), then choose the calibrator group.
  2. Read the fold change for each group.
  3. For efficiency, enter the template amount and mean Ct for each dilution point.
  4. Read the slope, efficiency, and the pass-or-check verdict.

Good to know

Runs offline. Methods: Livak and Schmittgen 2001; Pfaffl 2001. Confirm against your instrument's own software before reporting clinical results.
